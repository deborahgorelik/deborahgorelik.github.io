Favorite User Experience Professionals: What they are known for: Books they wrote
Don Norman Author of 'Design of Everyday Things' and coined the term HCI. He is currently the director of The Design Lab at UC San Diego. Norman is a pivotal in the field of User Experience. He is also known for his contribution to the Neilson Norman Group, which is a UX consulting firm. At 83 years old, he continues to give speeches, speak in podcasts, and write journals.
Steve Krug Author of 'Don't Make Me Think!' Which is a pivotal text in usability. He runs a one-man consulting firm out of Massachusetts. He is most famous for his books, which are used to teach usability across disciplines.
David Kelley Founder of the design firm IDEO, an internation design and consulting firm. Kelley is also a professor at Stanford's d.school. Kelley briefly worked at Boeing before starting his master's at Stanford. He wrote a book with his brother called 'Creative Confidence' which explains how creativity lies within each individual.
